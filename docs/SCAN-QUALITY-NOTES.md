# Scan-quality notes: pages worth re-scanning

> Compiled 2026-06-19 while translating `source/Hinglaj Puran.pdf`. The whole PDF is a photographed scan of a 1915 letterpress book, most of it is legible, but a number of pages have heavy ink-bleed, gutter-shadow, or faint impressions that made names and Sanskrit shlokas hard to read by eye. A **flat, even-lit, higher-DPI re-scan** of the pages below would most improve the translation's accuracy.
>
> **Page numbering:** the book's *printed* page = PDF page − offset, where the offset drifts between **+4 and +6** through the volume (front matter + an inserted plate). Both numbers are given so you can find the page either in the physical book (printed #) or the PDF (PDF #).

## General
- The **title page, the Brahmāṇī/Gāyatrī plate, and the TOC (PDF 1–5)** scanned cleanly.
- If re-scanning selectively, **Priority 1 + 2** are the ones that would actually change the translation. Priority 3 is for completeness only.
- A re-scan at **300 dpi, flattened (no gutter curl), grayscale, even lighting** is ideal; the current scans appear to be lower-dpi phone/photocopy captures.
