# Hinglaj Puran: Glossary & Dramatis Personae

> Every deity, asura, sage, king, place, community and key term in the grantha, with a one-line identification and the chapter(s) where it figures. Built to feed the site (search, captions, reel labels, the reader-archive). This is **itihaas (sacred history)**; names marked (?) await pandit review.

## The Goddess & her forms
- **Hingulā / Hingulāmbikā / Hinglaj Mātā**: the supreme Goddess as she arose at Hingul (Sindhu region); kuldevi of the Brahmakṣatriya. *(ch 64–66, 84)*
- **Mahāmāyā / Yoganidrā**: the cosmic Shakti / divine sleep who binds and releases Vishnu. *(ch 14–20)*
- **Mahālakṣmī · Mahākālī · Mahāsaraswatī**: the three supreme forms; the world arises from Mahālakṣmī. *(ch 2–3)*
- **Durgā**: the Goddess; also her name won by slaying the asura Durga. *(ch 4)*
- **Chaṇḍikā / Chāmuṇḍā**: the fierce battle-form who slays the demons. *(ch 8, 40, 57–62)*
- **Ambikā / Bhavānī / Gaurī / Pārvatī**: the Goddess as Shiva's consort. *(throughout)*
- **The Navadurgā** (ch 11): Nandā · Raktadantikā · Shākambharī · Durgā · Bhīmā · Bhrāmarī · Kālikā · Shivadūtī · Aindrī.
- **The Mātṛkās** (ch 9): Brāhmaṇī · Māheshwarī · Kaumārī · Vaiṣṇavī · Vārāhī · Aindrī · Chāmuṇḍā.
- **The Kumārī forms** (ch 8): Tripurā · Kalyāṇī · Rohiṇī · Kālī · Chaṇḍikā · Shāmbhavī · Durgā · Subhadrā (ages 2–10).
- **Gāyatrī-Sāvitrī**: invoked in the Maṅgalācharaṇa and the Sāvitrī-vrat. *(ch 63)*
- **Saraswatī**: mother of the Sāraswata lineage. *(ch 74–75)*

## The Trimūrti & gods
- **Brahmā**: creator; grantor of the demons' boons; narrates much of Pravāh 1's cosmology.
- **Vishnu** (Hari · Janārdana · Nārāyaṇa · Vāsudeva), preserver; takes the **Hayagrīva** (horse-headed) form (ch 14).
- **Shiva** (Rudra · Shankar · Mahādeva · Pashupati · Umāpati · Nīlakaṇṭha), destroyer; grants Parashurāma his boon (ch 73).
- **Indra**: king of the gods, exiled by the demons.
- **Bṛhaspati**: the gods' guru; war-counsellor (ch 48).
- **Gaṇapati**: Pārvatī's body-born son who slays Sindurāsura (ch 65).

## Asuras (demons)
- **Hingul** & **Sindur (Sindurāsura)**: sons of **Viprachitti**; Hingul, consumed by the Goddess's light, is granted liberation and her name. *(ch 65–66)*
- **Madhu & Kaiṭabha**: born of Vishnu's ear-wax in his yoga-nidrā; slain by Vishnu. *(ch 15–18)*
- **Hayagrīva** (asura), boon: slayable only by a horse-headed being; killed by Vishnu-Hayagrīva. *(ch 14)*
- **Mahishāsura (Mahisha)**: the buffalo-demon; boon that no deva/daitya/male can slay him → slain by the Devi. *(ch 46–62)*
- **Mahisha's generals:** **Durmukha** (ch 57) · **Chikṣura** & **Tāmrākṣa** (ch 58) · **Asilomā** & **Biḍāla** (ch 59–60). Referenced: Raktabīja, Dhūmralochana, Chaṇḍa-Muṇḍa.
- **Durga** (asura), boon that no *male* may defeat him; slain by the Goddess, who takes his name. *(ch 4)*
- **Bhīmāsura**: seizes Swarga, enslaves the gods; slain by the Devi. *(ch 72)*

## Sages & narrators
- **Vyāsa**: narrates Pravāh 1 to King Janamejaya.
- **Sūta / Sūta-Purāṇi**: narrates to the rishis at Naimishāraṇya.
- **Nārada** & **Nārāyaṇa-muni**: the dialogue that frames the Hingula tīrtha glory. *(ch 67)*
- **Dadhīchi**: sage near Mount Hingulā; his bones forge Indra's Vajra(?); shelters refugees. *(ch 70, 72, 74, 81)*
- **Jamadagni**: Parashurāma's father; owner of Kāmadhenu. *(ch 73, 76)*
- **Vasiṣṭha** & **Vishvāmitra**: the Nandinī (wish-cow) dispute; brahma-tej vs kshatra-tej. *(ch 34, 69)*
- **Bharadvāja**: shelters the exiled Sudarshana. *(ch 32–33)*
- **Gautama**: gives the princes their mantra-dīkṣā. *(ch 83)*
- **Lomasha**: proclaims Shakti supreme at Naimishāraṇya. *(ch 27)*
- **The Prajāpatis**: Marīchi · Atri · Pulastya · Dakṣa · (→ **Kashyapa**), in the cosmogony. *(ch 30)*
- **Mārkaṇḍeya**: referenced in the Mahishāsura account.

## Kings & mortals
- **Janamejaya**: Parikṣit's son; the king to whom Vyāsa narrates.
- **Parashurāma** (Bhārgava · Rāma, son of Jamadagni), annihilates the Kshatriyas; founds Rāmtīrth. *(ch 73, 76–84)*
- **Reṇukā**: Parashurāma's mother. *(ch 73, 77)*
- **Kārtavīrya Arjuna / Sahasrārjuna**: king of **Mahishmati**; steals Kāmadhenu. *(ch 76, 78)*
- **Jayasena**: progenitor-king of the Brahmakṣatriya; rules at **Kanauj**. *(ch 84–86)*
- **Bindhumān · Vishāl · Chandrashāl** (+ a fifth), the surviving princes with Jayasena. *(ch 81)*
- **Dhruvasandhi**: solar-dynasty king; father of Sudarshana. *(ch 31)*
- **Manoramā** & **Līlāvatī**: his two queens (Sudarshana's & Shatrujit's mothers). *(ch 31–42)*
- **Sudarshana**: exiled prince; master of the *klīṃ* bīja; regains his throne by the Devi's grace. *(ch 32–42)*
- **Sudhajit** & **Shatrujit**: the usurping rivals, slain by Chaṇḍikā. *(ch 33–40)*
- **Shashikalā**: princess of Kāshi who chooses Sudarshana. *(ch 35–41)*
- **Virasena**: the Kāshi king. *(ch 36)*
- **Ratnasena**: king who takes refuge from Parashurāma; later slain. *(ch 79, 81)*
- **Satyavrata**: the simpleton made a great poet by the Devi's grace. *(ch 27–28)*
- **Mandodarī**: princess of Siṃhala who refuses marriage for tapas. *(ch 61)*
- **Chandrasena** & **Guṇavatī**: her parents. *(ch 61)*
- **Durgadeva**: Sāraswata brahmin who mediates the Lohana origin. *(ch 86)*
- **Jayachand**: Kanauj king in the community-origin account. *(ch 86)*
- **Vajranābh(?)**: Krishna's great-grandson, referenced in the closing. *(seed; verify)*

## Places & tīrthas
- **Hingulā / Hinguladri / Hingulā Pīṭh**: the sacred mountain & shrine in the **Sindhu** region (Balochistan).
- **Hemagiri**: where Mahisha performs tapas. **Meru / Sumeru**: the cosmic mountain.
- **Mandākinī & Hingulā rivers**: born of Brahmā's kumbha (ch 68); **Parāshara** river (ch 72).
- **Rāmtīrth / Rāmkṣetra**: founded by Parashurāma; the **Muktigaṅgā** river (from Shiva's tears) & the **Bindeshwar/Rāmeshwar** liṅga. *(ch 73)*
- **Nāga-tīrth · Koṭitīrth · Puṇyadā(?)-tīrth**: sin-destroying tīrthas of the region. *(ch 67–72)*
- **Pushkar (near Ajmer)**: sanctifies the Pushkarṇā brahmins (ch 86). **Kanauj**: Jayasena's seat. **Mahishmati**: Sahasrārjuna's city. **Narmadā**: the cow-theft river.
- In the ākhyānas: **Ayodhyā · Kāshi/Banaras · Siṃhala**. Cosmic loci: **Naimishāraṇya · Badarikāshrama · Satyaloka · Vaikuṇṭha · Kailāsa**.

## Communities (the kuldevi tradition)
- **Brahmakṣatriya**: the surviving Kshatriyas hidden as brahmins under Hingulā's law. *(ch 84–86)*
- **Lohana**: from Jayasena's 48 Rāṭhoḍ sardārs (from *loha*, iron/weapons). *(ch 86)*
- **Bhāṭiā**: from the Bhāṭi Rajput line. **Khatri**: a trading branch. **Pushkarṇā Brāhmins**: sanctified by Rāma at Pushkar. *(ch 86)*

## Key terms
- **Itihaas**: sacred history (how this grantha is to be regarded). **Pravāh**: stream/major section. **Adhyāy**: chapter.
- **Kuldevi**: clan/lineage goddess. **Shakti**: the Goddess as the power animating all gods.
- **Tejas**: the gods' combined radiance/wrath that forms the Devi (ch 52).
- **Klīṃ**: the Goddess's bīja-mantra (Sudarshana's). **Navākṣarī**: the nine-syllable Chaṇḍikā mantra.
- **Saguṇa / Nirguṇa**: the Goddess with/without attributes (ch 24). **The three guṇas**: sattva, rajas, tamas (ch 25).
- **Akhaṇḍ jyot**: the eternal flame. **The vow:** *प्राण जाये पर वचन न जाये* ("the life may go, but not the given word").
- Rites: **Vrat · Yajna · Homa · Nyāsa · Puraścharaṇa · Kalasha-sthāpanā · Kumārī-pūjā · Balidāna · Visarjana**.
- **Vardān** (boon) · **Svayaṃvara** · **Satī** · **Tapas** · **Sharaṇ** (refuge) · **Darshan**.
