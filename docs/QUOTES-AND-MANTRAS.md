## Mantras

**Hingulā mantra** *(per STYLE.md / tradition)*
> ॐ हिंगुले परमहिंगुले अमृतरूपिणि तनुशक्तिमनः शिवे श्री हिंगुलाय नमः
> *Oṃ Hiṅgule Paramahiṅgule Amṛtarūpiṇi Tanuśaktimanaḥ Śive Śrī Hiṅgulāya Namaḥ*

**Navākṣarī — the nine-syllable Chaṇḍikā mantra** *(grantha ch 6 cites "the nine-syllable mantra"; the canonical form is)*
> ॐ ऐं ह्रीं क्लीं चामुण्डायै विच्चे
> *Oṃ Aiṃ Hrīṃ Klīṃ Chāmuṇḍāyai Vichche* — *(verify the grantha's exact wording)*

**Klīṃ** — the bīja of Hingulā/Devī devotion; the seed-mantra by which the boy **Sudarshana** wins the Goddess's grace *(ch 34–35)*.

**Bīja-mantras** — Hrīṃ and the seed-syllables taught with the nyāsa *(ch 6)*.

## The Vow
> प्राण जाये पर वचन न जाये
> *Prāṇ jāye par vachan na jāye* — **"The life may go, but not the given word."**
> The Charan / warrior-devotional creed — *veer + bhakti*, the blade and the vow.

## Key verses & lines from the grantha

**The invocation** *(ch 65, opening)*
> *"namo hiṅgule devi mātar...namaste namaste namaste yoginām yogagamye"*
> "Salutations to you, O Goddess Hingulā, O Mother... salutations, salutations, thrice salutations — O you whom the yogis attain through yoga."

**The Maṅgalācharaṇa prose** *(printed p.1)*
> "O Hingulā — you of the unbroken divine radiance, the world's origin, embodiment of Mahālakṣmī; at your lotus-feet I bow. O Mother of the World, you dwell in the hearts of those who remember your name, destroy their delusion, and are in every way the bestower of welfare."

**The bridge-vision of Hingulā** *(ch 64)*
> *"raktākṣī raktavāmbujā jātā sā hiṅgulāmbikā; devānāṃ rakṣaṇārthāya āvirbhūtā svayambikā"*
> "Red-eyed, born of a red lotus, she arose as Hingulāmbikā; to protect the gods she manifested, self-arisen."

**The naming** *(ch 66)* — the heart of the whole grantha
> "I have slain this demon; yet through the fame of my name he shall endure in the world. **From the name 'Hingulāsura,' my name shall be called Hingulā Devī.**"

**The phalashruti** *(ch 66)*
> *"tasyādhu chaṇḍikā devī prasannā syānna saṃśayaḥ"*
> "Upon whoever [hears this], Goddess Chaṇḍikā is pleased — of this there is no doubt."

**The Devi's dharma** *(ch 59, on the battlefield)*
> "I roam to protect the good (sādhu) and destroy the wicked (asādhu); the wicked Mahisha rose to slay the gods — so I have come for his slaying."

**The Śākta conviction** *(ch 17)*
> "Brahmā creates, Vishnu preserves, Umāpati destroys — but only when empowered by Shakti. **Shiva without Shakti is inert.**"

**Hingulā's law to her people** *(ch 84)*
> "Rule a thousand years; outwardly keep the brahmin rites; honour the Sāraswata brahmins — and **your lineage shall call me its mother (kuldevi)**."

**The sealing** *(ch 87)*
> *samāpto'yaṃ granthaḥ* — "This grantha is complete."
