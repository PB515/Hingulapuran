# The Narrative Timeline — the Arc of the Sacred History

> The grantha's sacred history (itihaas) as an ordered arc, for the landing-page story-flow and the scene-reel sequence. **Time here is cyclic, cosmic (kalpa) time, not dated chronology** — the order is narrative, not calendar. Chapter refs in (parentheses).

## The main arc — seven movements

### 1 · The Beginning — the Goddess as the ground of all *(ch 2, 30)*
Before all, **Mahālakṣmī**; from her the three powers are apportioned — Gaurī to Rudra, Lakṣmī to Vishnu, Saraswatī to Brahmā — so that the trimūrti can create, preserve and destroy. *"From Mahālakṣmī the whole moving-and-unmoving world arose."* Meru rises, the prajāpatis and lineages are born, the gods take their abodes, the ocean is churned. **The conviction the whole book rests on:** Shakti is the cause; the gods act only by her power.

### 2 · The First Charita — Hayagrīva & Madhu-Kaiṭabha *(ch 14–18)*
Vishnu lies in **yoga-nidrā**; his head is severed and restored as the horse-headed **Hayagrīva** to slay an asura of that name. The demons **Madhu & Kaiṭabha**, born of his ear-wax, threaten Brahmā; **Brahmā hymns Yoganidrā**, she releases Vishnu, and he **slays the two demons**. The Goddess as Mahāmāyā — she who binds and frees even Vishnu.

### 3 · The Devi Māhātmya — the slaying of Mahishāsura *(ch 46–64)*
The buffalo-demon **Mahishāsura** wins a boon that no god or man can kill him, conquers the three worlds, and exiles the gods. Their combined **tejas fuses into the Goddess** (Mahishāsura-mardinī), armed by every god. After the great war with his generals — Durmukha, Chikṣura, Tāmrākṣa, Asilomā, Biḍāla — she **beheads Mahishāsura**. *(Kindred victories: the asura Durga, ch 4; **Bhīmāsura**, ch 72.)* The earth is restored — and a bridging vision shows **Hingulāmbikā** rising.

### 4 · The Hingula Origin *(ch 65–72)*
In the country of Rāmbhaka, the rākṣasa Viprachitti's sons **Hingul & Sindur** conquer the gods; Pārvatī's body-born son **Gaṇapati** slays Sindur. Grieving, **Hingul** does a thousand-year tapas on Meru, wins Brahmā's boon, and seizes Swarga. The Goddess takes an **enchanting form**, lures him to the **Sindhu ocean**, and as he merges into her crying *"Devī!"* he dies and **attains liberation**. She decrees: *"From 'Hingulāsura,' my name shall be Hingulā Devī,"* and the Sindhu tīrtha becomes **Hingulā**. The holy places of the region are established (ch 67–72).

### 5 · The Parashurāma Age *(ch 73–83)*
**Sahasrārjuna** of Mahishmati seizes Jamadagni's wish-cow **Kāmadhenu**, and Jamadagni is slain. His son **Parashurāma** vows vengeance, and in the **Rāma–Arjuna war** and his wider campaign sweeps the earth **niḥkṣatriya** (empty of Kshatriyas). At Mount Hingulā he wins Shiva's boon; **Rāmtīrth** is founded (the Muktigaṅgā, the Bindeshwar liṅga).

### 6 · The Refuge & the Community Origins *(ch 84–86)*
The surviving princes take **refuge at Hingulā**. She grants her **vardān and law** — rule a thousand years, keep brahmin rites outwardly, honour the Sāraswata brahmins, *"call me your mother."* Through **Jayasena's** dīkṣā the survivors become the **Brahmakṣatriya**, branching into **Lohana, Bhāṭiā, Khatri** and the **Pushkarṇā Brāhmins** — a warrior people made traders, bound to Hingulā as kuldevi.

### 7 · The Sealing *(ch 87)*
Sūta recapitulates the whole account — from the Goddess's origin through Mahishāsura, Hingula and Parashurāma to the community lineages — and the grantha is closed: *samāpto'yaṃ granthaḥ.*

## Inset tales (told *within* the arc, not part of the cosmic sequence)
- **Sudarshana** *(ch 31–42)* — the exiled prince who masters the *klīṃ* bīja and regains his throne by the Devi.
- **Satyavrata** *(ch 27–28)* — the simpleton made a great poet by the Devi's grace.
- **Ratnasena** *(ch 79, 81)* — the king who takes refuge from Parashurāma.
- **Mandodarī** *(ch 61)* — the Siṃhala princess who chose tapas over marriage.
- **Vishvāmitra–Nandinī** *(ch 34)* — brahma-tej over kshatra-tej.

> **For the site:** movements 3 (tejas → Mahishāsura) and 4 (Hingula) are the flagship reel beats; the seven-movement arc is the natural backbone for a scroll-through "story of the grantha."
